Interesting Facts About CAMEL

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat

Interesting Facts About CHAMELEON

  1. UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN’T REGROW THEIR TAILS.
  2. SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL.
  3. THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.

Interesting Facts About PIGEON

  1. THEY MIGHT BE THE FIRST DOMESTICATED BIRD.
  2. THEY UNDERSTAND SPACE AND TIME.
  3. THEY SAVED THOUSANDS OF HUMAN LIVES DURING WORLD WARS I AND II.

Interesting Facts About TEDDY BEAR

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.

Interesting Facts About PANDA

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  3. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting Facts About FLYING CICADA

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. Their predators, or animals that will eat them, don’t know they’re coming. Lots of animals, including birds, mice and raccoons, are in for a bug surprise!
